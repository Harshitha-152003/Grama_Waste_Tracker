Detailed Description: Grama Waste Tracker is an innovative smart village cleanliness and waste management application developed to improve sanitation monitoring and promote environmental responsibility in rural and semi-urban areas. The application acts as a “Digital Waste Supervisor” that helps citizens, sanitation workers, and gram panchayat authorities work together to maintain village cleanliness efficiently. Through the app, users can report issues such as garbage accumulation, overflowing public bins, missed waste collection, blocked drainage systems, roadside littering, and illegal waste dumping. Citizens can upload photos, enter descriptions, and share the location of the issue directly through their smartphones, making the reporting process simple and accessible for everyone. The application provides real-time complaint tracking and status updates so that users can monitor whether the issue has been assigned, is under progress, or has been resolved. By combining technology with community participation, Grama Waste Tracker creates a transparent and organized waste management system that encourages cleanliness, accountability, and sustainable environmental practices in villages. The app also helps local authorities analyze sanitation performance, identify frequently affected areas, and plan effective waste collection strategies to maintain a healthy and pollution-free environment.

Problem Statement: Waste management remains one of the major challenges faced by many villages and rural communities in Karnataka. Improper disposal of household waste, delayed garbage collection, open dumping of waste, and poor drainage maintenance often lead to severe environmental and public health problems. Accumulated waste creates unpleasant odors, attracts insects and animals, contaminates water sources, and increases the spread of diseases such as dengue, malaria, and other infections. In many villages, sanitation complaints are still communicated manually through verbal reporting or paperwork, which causes delays in action and lack of proper tracking. Citizens often do not know whether their complaints have been addressed, while local authorities struggle to manage multiple sanitation issues efficiently without a centralized system. Small gram panchayats may also lack the technological infrastructure required to monitor cleanliness activities effectively. Therefore, there is a strong need for a simple, affordable, and user-friendly digital solution that can help citizens report waste-related issues quickly, allow authorities to monitor complaints systematically, and improve coordination between sanitation workers and local administration. Grama Waste Tracker addresses these problems by providing a smart and organized platform for complaint management, waste tracking, and sanitation monitoring.

Tools & Technologies: The Grama Waste Tracker application is developed using modern Android development technologies to ensure smooth performance, scalability, and an intuitive user experience. Kotlin is used as the primary programming language because of its modern syntax, safety features, and compatibility with Android development. Jetpack Compose is used for designing responsive and interactive user interfaces, while Material Design 3 provides a clean, modern, and visually appealing layout following Google’s latest UI standards. The application follows the MVVM (Model-View-ViewModel) architecture pattern to separate business logic from the user interface, making the code more maintainable and scalable. Room Database and SQLite are implemented for local storage of complaints, user details, and sanitation records, allowing offline functionality and efficient data management. StateFlow and Kotlin Flow are used for handling real-time data updates and reactive programming, while ViewModel ensures data survives configuration changes such as screen rotation. Kotlin Coroutines are used for efficient background processing and asynchronous tasks without affecting app performance. Firebase Authentication is integrated for secure login and user management, and Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) is used to send instant notifications and status updates to users and sanitation workers. Navigation Compose helps manage screen transitions smoothly, while RecyclerView, DiffUtil, and ListAdapter are used for displaying dynamic complaint lists efficiently. Android Studio, Gradle, Android SDK, Git, and GitHub are used during development for coding, dependency management, version control, and project collaboration.

App Usage and User Flow: The Grama Waste Tracker application is designed with a simple and easy-to-understand workflow so that users from rural and semi-urban areas can use it comfortably without technical difficulties. The process begins with user registration and secure login, where citizens, sanitation workers, and administrators can create accounts using their mobile numbers or email authentication. After logging in, citizens can access the complaint reporting section to submit waste-related problems. Users can upload photos of garbage dumps, overflowing bins, dirty streets, drainage blockages, or uncollected waste, along with a short description and the exact location of the issue. Once the complaint is submitted, the system stores the information and notifies the concerned sanitation authority or worker. The complaint then appears on the admin dashboard, where authorities can review the issue, assign cleaning tasks to workers, and monitor progress. Users receive real-time status updates such as “Complaint Received,” “Worker Assigned,” “Cleaning in Progress,” and “Issue Resolved.” The application also provides notifications about daily garbage collection schedules, village cleanliness campaigns, recycling awareness drives, and sanitation announcements. Administrators can view statistics such as total complaints, resolved issues, pending cases, and worker performance reports, helping them improve waste management operations efficiently. The app creates better communication between citizens and local authorities while increasing transparency in sanitation services.

Technical Implementation and Highlights: The Grama Waste Tracker application includes several smart technical features that improve efficiency, performance, and usability. The complaint management system is built using Room Database and Firebase, allowing secure storage of complaint details, uploaded images, user records, and complaint history. A smart priority-based logic engine is implemented using if-else and when conditions to classify complaints based on severity. For example, medical waste, blocked drainage systems, or large garbage accumulations are automatically marked as high-priority issues requiring immediate attention. The application also includes a color-coded complaint status system where green indicates resolved issues, orange represents work in progress, and red highlights urgent or pending complaints. Push notifications are implemented using Firebase Cloud Messaging to provide instant alerts whenever a complaint is submitted, assigned, updated, or resolved. Offline support is another important feature of the application, allowing users to create complaints even without internet access. The data is stored locally and synchronized automatically when connectivity becomes available. The app also supports image uploads, location tracking, real-time data observation through StateFlow, and efficient background processing through Kotlin Coroutines. Proper navigation management and optimized UI rendering ensure smooth performance even on low-end Android devices commonly used in rural areas. These technical implementations make the system reliable, scalable, and user-friendly for both citizens and administrators.

Impact Goals: The primary goal of Grama Waste Tracker is to improve cleanliness, sanitation, and public health in villages and rural communities by using technology-driven waste management solutions. The application aims to reduce pollution, prevent disease outbreaks, and create healthier living conditions by ensuring timely waste collection and proper sanitation monitoring. By encouraging citizens to actively report waste-related issues, the app increases community participation and public awareness regarding cleanliness and environmental responsibility. The system also strengthens communication between villagers, sanitation workers, and gram panchayat authorities, resulting in faster problem resolution and improved transparency. Another major impact goal is supporting digital governance in rural areas by helping local administrations manage sanitation activities efficiently through real-time monitoring and organized complaint tracking. The application also contributes to environmental sustainability by promoting responsible waste disposal, cleaner surroundings, and awareness about recycling practices. Through better waste management and citizen involvement, Grama Waste Tracker supports the vision of smart villages, sustainable rural development, and a cleaner Karnataka while helping local communities maintain a healthier and safer environment for future generations.
